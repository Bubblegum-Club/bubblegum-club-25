Studio44 Mocaplab

BRIEF:
We were tasked with reimagining the visual identity and online presence of Studio44 Mocap Lab— a cutting-edge motion capture studio established by Swiss dance company Gilles Jobin,known for its innovative work at the intersection of technology, movement, and performance.

INSIGHT
As Studio44 Mocap Lab expanded its vision at the intersection of motion capture, choreography,and immersive digital performance, we crafted a visual language that draws from the aesthetics ofmotion data and embodied technology. The result is a design system that communicates fluidity, precision, and transformation — echoing the lab’s pioneering role in movement-ledinnovation.

DELIVERED:
Developed a brand concept that reflects Studio44’s hybrid identity as a tech-forward, movement-driven space Designed a logo and visual system inspired by the dynamics of motion capture and digital embodiment Built a new website aligned with the lab’s forward-thinking ethos and user needsCreated a social media strategy to connect with a global, digitally literate arts and tech audienceShaped and implemented a launch strategy to ensure the refreshed identity rolled out seamlesslyacross platforms

ROSALUXEMBURGFOUNDATION

BRIEF:
We were commissioned by the Rosa Luxemburg Foundation in Geneva to develop a podcastcommemorating the 75th anniversary of the Universal Declaration of Human Rights. Our missionwas to create a platform that resonates with audiences in the Global South, appealing not only toprofessionals but also to young, everyday listeners.

APPROACH
We began with in-depth research into the podcast landscape across the Global South, with afocus on the African continent. By studying the most popular youth-oriented podcasts, weidentified that personal storytelling, paired with accessible language, was the most effective way toengage and educate audiences on critical issues like human rights

Shap Shap

BRIEF
‍Over a four-year period, Association Shap Shap commissioned us to develop a new visual identity, website, and social media strategy to strengthen its presence and position it as a leading voice at the intersection of culture and social justice. The goal was to build a distinct and recognisable brand that resonates with both public sector organisations and its youth-focused audiences.

APPROACH
‍We defined Shap Shap’s core brand essence and tone of voice — grounded in confidence, fluidity, self-definition, and play — to position it as a mirror and amplifier of youth culture with pan-African relevance.Drawing inspiration from Shap Shap’s core pillars — live art, music, and street art — and benchmarking against organisations operating in similar spaces, we developed a cohesive framework guiding content themes, visual direction, and audience engagement. This ensured Shap Shap’s identity reflected both its cultural roots and its social impact mission.
